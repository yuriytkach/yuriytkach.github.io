Few Words About Drones 🛰️ and Their Batteries 🔋

Published May 02, 2022

Back to all posts

I think many of you have seen videos of the successful use of various drones by our heroic warriors! They use them to conduct reconnaissance, correct fire, detect clusters of enemies, drop shells, and do many other interesting things.

In addition to specialized devices such as Bayraktar, the guys are quite effective in using small commercial drones, such as DJI Mavic. They are not very expensive, easy to use, and the benefits are great: they help unnoticeable to see who sits where. Of course, with Chinese commercial drones, you need to keep in mind the safety of use, so that suddenly the enemy does not follow you back, but I know that our soldiers are smart and understand all this. 😉

However, it is not enough to have a drone and be able to use it. The fact is that it flies on a single charge for about 40 minutes. And so, imagine, you got closer to the enemy, sat in the bushes, launched a drone, flew for half an hour, and then what? Run back to charge? You can’t run here and there, and it's dangerous. So you need spare batteries. And a lot. So you can fly during the day and only return to the location in the evening.

📦 I am buying spare batteries for drones for our soldiers, who are currently fighting in Luhansk region. The first batch has already been delivered to them, but more is needed. Moreover, the guys themselves have got extra drones of different models, so we need different batteries.

Batteries are still available in various stores, they cost from 120 to 200 euros, depending on the model of the drone. We need to raise around 2000 euros for the purchase of 15 pieces.

Along with the walkie-talkies, which I wrote about in the last post, I am raising funds for additional batteries. Drones are not only extra eyes, but in the skillful hands of our soldiers they can cause a lot of trouble to the enemy. But I will write about it in the next post. 😉

Know that each donation, regardless of size, brings us closer to achieving our goal.

Thank you to everyone who joins!

Together we are a force!
